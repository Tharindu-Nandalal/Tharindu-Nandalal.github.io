Tharindu Nandalal

PhD Researcher · Institute for Engineering and Computational Mechanics (ITM) · University of Stuttgart

I am a PhD student working on computational and experimental modelling of classical guitars. My research focuses on finite element modelling of guitar soundboards, structural dynamics, and the influence of wood material variability on acoustic performance.

Research

FE Modelling of Guitar Soundboards

Development of parametric finite element models for classical guitar top plates, including morphing mesh strategies and modal analysis.

Wood Material Variability

Investigating the effect of natural wood property variability on the vibroacoustic behaviour of guitar soundboards using sampling and uncertainty quantification methods.

Experimental Validation

Laser Doppler vibrometry and data acquisition for experimental modal analysis, used to validate and update simulation models.

Soundboard Optimisation

Optimisation of guitar plate geometry and material distribution to achieve target acoustic properties.
