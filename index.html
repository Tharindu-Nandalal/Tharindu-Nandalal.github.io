PhD Researcher in Computational Mechanics

Institute for Engineering and Computational Mechanics (ITM), University of Stuttgart

I am a PhD student working on computational and experimental modelling of classical guitars. My research focuses on parametric finite element modelling of guitar soundboards, structural dynamics, and the influence of wood material variability on vibroacoustic performance.

Featured Publications

Geometrically parameterized reduced-order finite element model for guitar soundboard shape optimization

Nandalal, T. D.; Cillo, P.; Ziegler, P.; Eberhard, P.

Acta Acustica, 2025

A parametric reduced-order finite element model is developed for the shape optimisation of classical guitar soundboards. The approach combines geometrically parameterized meshes with model order reduction techniques to enable efficient exploration of the design space while preserving vibroacoustic accuracy.
